# Arthur Brand Voice Guide

## Core Voice Attributes
- **Authoritative**: Arthur speaks as an operational peer who has deployed AI governance at scale with large enterprise clients. Not an outside consultant or academic.
- **Practitioner-level**: Write for senior leaders (CIOs, CISOs, CCOs) who understand their domain deeply. No beginner explainers.
- **Confident without arrogance**: We know our platform is best-in-class. We let the capabilities and results speak, not superlatives.
- **Technical but accessible**: Use precise technical language where appropriate, but never jargon for jargon's sake.

## Tone Rules
- Short paragraphs. No fluff. Every sentence earns its place.
- Lead with the problem or insight, not with Arthur.
- Use concrete specifics over vague claims. Numbers, use cases, regulatory citations > adjectives.
- Avoid: hype, buzzwords, "AI will change everything" framing, fear-mongering.
- Avoid: "cutting-edge," "revolutionary," "game-changing," "next-generation."
- Never salesy. Never desperate. Never begging for attention.

## Writing Conventions
- Use active voice.
- Prefer "you" over "organizations" or "enterprises" — speak directly to the reader.
- Use em dashes for emphasis — sparingly.
- Oxford comma: yes.
- Numbers: spell out one through nine, use numerals for 10+.
- Acronyms: spell out on first use, then abbreviate.

## What Arthur Is / Is Not
- IS: An AI governance platform. An operational control plane for AI agents. A system of record for agent inventory, policies, and audit trails.
- IS NOT: A consulting firm. A compliance certifier. A replacement for human judgment on policy decisions.

## Compliance Language Guardrails
- NEVER say Arthur "makes you compliant" with any regulation.
- DO say Arthur "provides capabilities that support your compliance program."
- NEVER guarantee regulatory outcomes.
- DO reference specific regulatory frameworks factually (FINRA, SEC, OCC, FFIEC) with accurate citations.
- NEVER name specific financial institution clients unless explicitly approved.
- NEVER mention pricing.
