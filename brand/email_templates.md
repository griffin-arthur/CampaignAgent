# Arthur Email Templates & Guidelines

## General Email Rules
- Subject lines: 6-10 words. No ALL CAPS. No exclamation marks. A/B test every send with two variants.
- Preview text: 40-90 characters. Complements (not repeats) the subject line.
- Body: 150-250 words max. 3-5 short paragraphs. One clear CTA per email.
- Tone: Like a smart colleague forwarding you something relevant — not a marketing blast.
- Sign-off: Keep it clean. "— The Arthur Team" or individual sender name if from an exec.

## Nurture Sequence Structure (3-Email)

### Email 1: Problem Awareness
- Open with a question or surprising stat that hits the reader's pain point.
- Frame the problem in their world (not Arthur's product world).
- Soft CTA: link to thought leadership content (blog, column, report).
- No product pitch.

### Email 2: Regulatory / Market Urgency
- Escalate urgency with a concrete external trigger (regulatory change, market shift, analyst report).
- Connect the trigger to the problem from Email 1.
- Bridge to solution category (not product yet).
- Soft CTA: deeper content or early demo nudge.

### Email 3: Solution + Hard CTA
- Now introduce Arthur's platform as the answer.
- Lead with capabilities, not features. What can they DO with Arthur?
- Social proof if available (anonymized case references, analyst validation).
- Hard CTA: Book a demo. Single, clear, unmissable.

## Formatting
- Use line breaks between paragraphs (not indentation).
- Bold the CTA link text.
- No images in nurture emails (deliverability).
- Plain text feel — minimal HTML styling.
