# Arthur Social Media Guidelines (LinkedIn Focus)

## Voice on LinkedIn
- Slightly less formal than blog content, but still authoritative.
- Write as a knowledgeable peer sharing insights — not a brand broadcasting announcements.
- OK to be conversational. Short sentences. Sentence fragments for emphasis.
- Avoid: hashtag spam (max 3-5 per post), emoji overuse, corporate jargon.

## Organic Post Types & Angles
Rotate across these angles for variety:

1. **Stat-led**: Open with a compelling data point. Source it. Then provide the "so what" for the reader's world.
2. **Question-led**: Pose a provocative question the audience can't easily answer. Drive engagement through comments.
3. **Narrative-led**: Short story or scenario. "Imagine your examiner asks for X. Could you produce it today?"
4. **CTA-led**: Direct value proposition. What Arthur does → why it matters → link.

## LinkedIn Organic Specs
- Character limit: 3,000 (but aim for 500-1,200 for engagement).
- First 2-3 lines are critical — they appear before "See more." Hook early.
- Line breaks between every 1-2 sentences for mobile readability.
- Max 1 link per post (LinkedIn deprioritizes posts with links — consider putting link in first comment for organic reach).
- Images/carousels boost reach but are not required.

## Sponsored Content Ads
- **Headline**: Max 70 characters. Lead with the reader's problem or a provocative claim.
- **Body**: 100-150 words. Problem → bridge → CTA. No fluff.
- **CTA button**: Use "Learn More" for awareness, "Book a Demo" for conversion campaigns.

## Hashtag Strategy
- Always include: #AIGovernance
- Rotate among: #AgenticAI #FinancialServices #AICompliance #EnterpriseAI #CISO #AIRisk
- Max 5 hashtags per post.

## Engagement Rules
- Posts from executive accounts outperform company page posts — prioritize exec ghostwriting.
- Engagement-only posts (no link, just a question) should be 1 in every 4 posts.
