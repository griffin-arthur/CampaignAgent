## 2. Target Audience

This campaign targets three personas within financial institutions. All messaging should resonate across roles, but individual assets can skew toward one persona where noted.

**Persona A — Head of AI / CIO**
- Senior technology leader responsible for AI strategy and infrastructure decisions.
- Motivation: Needs to scale agentic AI adoption without creating ungovernable risk. Under pressure from the board to show AI ROI while maintaining operational control.
- Pain point: Agents are proliferating across business units on different stacks (Bedrock, Vertex, custom). No single pane of glass for what's running, who owns it, or what policies apply.

**Persona B — CISO / Head of Security**
- Responsible for enterprise security posture, including non-human identities and data access controls.
- Motivation: Shadow agents are a new attack surface. Needs to discover unauthorized agents, enforce least-privilege access, and produce audit trails for examiners.
- Pain point: Traditional security tools weren't built for autonomous AI agents that reason, call tools, and access sensitive systems. Can't apply the same controls used for static software.

**Persona C — Chief Compliance Officer / Head of Risk**
- Responsible for regulatory compliance (SEC, FINRA, OCC, FFIEC) and enterprise risk management.
- Motivation: FINRA's 2026 Regulatory Oversight Report now includes a dedicated GenAI section. SEC examination priorities explicitly cover AI governance, supervision, and data protection. Needs to demonstrate auditable governance before examiners arrive.
- Pain point: Can't produce a comprehensive inventory of AI agents, their data access patterns, or the policies enforced on them. No audit trail connecting agent decisions to accountable owners.

## 3. Key Messages

Lead with the general ADG value proposition, then layer in financial-services-specific regulatory urgency.

**Primary message:** You can't govern AI agents you can't see. Arthur's Agent Discovery & Governance platform automatically discovers every agent across your environment — regardless of stack or cloud provider — and gives you a unified control plane for guardrails, evaluation, access management, and audit trails.

**Supporting messages (rotate across assets):**

1. The shadow agent crisis is a regulatory crisis in financial services. FINRA's 2026 report now explicitly addresses GenAI and agentic AI governance. SEC examination priorities cover AI supervision and data protection. Waiting for an incident to build governance means building it under regulatory scrutiny.

2. Discovery is the foundation — but governance is the requirement. Most enterprises have started inventorying agents. In financial services, inventory alone doesn't satisfy examiners. You need enforceable policies, continuous evaluation, and auditable decision trails.

3. One-size-fits-all governance fails in regulated industries. A customer-facing wealth advisory agent needs different guardrails than a back-office trade reconciliation agent. Arthur's platform lets you customize guardrails, evaluators, and access policies per use case — so compliance scales with your agent footprint.

4. Platform-agnostic governance eliminates blind spots. Whether your teams build on AWS Bedrock, Google Vertex AI, or custom frameworks, Arthur provides a single governance layer across every environment. No gaps, no fragmented policies, no shadow agents.

5. Governance is an accelerator, not a brake. The institutions that scale agentic AI fastest will be the ones that can demonstrate control to regulators, boards, and clients. Arthur makes governance the enabler — not the bottleneck.

## 6. Tone & Brand Constraints

- Tone: Authoritative and practitioner-level. Write for senior leaders who understand their regulatory obligations, not beginners. Avoid hype, buzzwords, and vague "AI will change everything" framing.
- Voice: Arthur speaks as an operational peer — the platform that has done this with large enterprise clients — not as an outside consultant. Confidence without arrogance.
- Constraints:
  - Only reference Arthur ADG capabilities that actually exist (see published content on arthur.ai).
  - Do not make specific compliance guarantees (e.g., "Arthur makes you FINRA-compliant"). Instead: "Arthur provides the discovery, governance, and audit trail capabilities that support your compliance program."
  - Do not mention pricing.
  - Do not name specific financial institution clients unless explicitly approved.
  - All regulatory references (FINRA 2026 report, SEC examination priorities) must be factually accurate and linked to primary sources where possible.
